Captain (IN) Anil Kumar (Retd.)

Principal Advisor – Naval Systems & Digital Shipyards

Nearly Three Decades of Service in the Indian Navy and the Ministry of Defence, with Expertise in Frontline Naval Operations, Indigenous Warship Development, Aircraft Carrier Projects, Defence Acquisition, Strategic Planning and International Defence Cooperation

Captain (IN) Anil Kumar (Retd.) brings nearly three decades of service in the Indian Navy and the Ministry of Defence, Government of India. A senior Executive Branch officer, he has held key appointments spanning frontline naval operations, indigenous warship development, Aircraft Carrier Projects, Naval Staff Qualitative Requirements (NSQRs), defence acquisition, strategic planning and international defence cooperation.

His career combines extensive operational experience with capability development, enabling him to bridge operational naval requirements with engineering and technological capabilities across complex naval shipbuilding projects. His expertise in naval platforms, indigenous shipbuilding, defence acquisition and maritime digital transformation provides a strong foundation for advising on AI-enabled Industrial Intelligence, Digital Twins and Digital Shipyard initiatives.

Naval Capability Development

Captain Anil Kumar has played a significant role in India's indigenous naval capability development through appointments involving the Directorate of Staff Requirements, Aircraft Carrier Projects and Naval Operations. His work focused on translating operational requirements into practical engineering and technological solutions while supporting indigenous defence manufacturing and long-term capability development.

Naval Capability Planning & Staff Requirements

During his tenure in the Directorate of Staff Requirements, Captain Anil Kumar was responsible for formulating Naval Staff Qualitative Requirements (NSQRs) for several mission-critical naval platforms and systems, including:

  • Integrated Bridge Systems (IBS)
  • Bridge Simulators
  • Automatic Identification Systems (AIS)
  • Fleet Support Ships
  • Naval Offshore Patrol Vessels (NOPVs)
  • Indigenous Aircraft Carrier

This work required balancing operational requirements, technical feasibility, lifecycle support, technology maturity and acquisition considerations while ensuring that user requirements were translated into practical capability outcomes.

Aircraft Carrier Projects

As Director – Aircraft Carrier Projects at Integrated Headquarters, Ministry of Defence (Navy), Captain Anil Kumar managed the Indigenous Aircraft Carrier (IAC-1) project and led the formulation of the Statement of Case for the next-generation Indigenous Aircraft Carrier (IAC-2). His responsibilities included coordinating Naval Headquarters, shipyards, design agencies and industry partners to support project execution, systems integration and capability development. This appointment provided first-hand experience of the coordination, configuration management and lifecycle challenges associated with complex naval shipbuilding projects.

Operational Experience

Captain Anil Kumar commanded two frontline Indian Naval warships and served as a specialist Navigation & Direction Officer and Operations Officer on frontline warships, gaining extensive operational sea experience across a wide range of naval missions.

As the Commissioning Navigating Officer of INS Bias, constructed at Garden Reach Shipbuilders & Engineers (GRSE), Kolkata, he was a member of the commissioning crew responsible for acceptance of the newly constructed warship on behalf of the Indian Navy. His responsibilities included validating the vessel against Qualitative/Technical Requirements, participating in harbour and sea trials, identifying operational deficiencies and working with the shipyard to resolve issues prior to commissioning.

He was also a member of the Indian Navy's acceptance team for an Indian Fleet Tanker constructed at Fincantieri Shipyard, Italy, participating in inspection, trials and acceptance of the vessel prior to induction into service.

These assignments provided first-hand exposure to the practical challenges of ship acceptance, evolving user requirements, configuration validation and operational readiness—issues that remain central to modern Digital Shipyard initiatives.

During his tenure at the Directorate of Naval Operations, he was responsible for operational planning, fleet coordination and maritime readiness. This operational perspective provides practical insight into the realities of operating, maintaining and sustaining complex naval platforms throughout their service life.

Defence Acquisition & Strategic Cooperation

As Director/Advisor (International Cooperation) in the Ministry of Defence, Captain Anil Kumar was responsible for strategic defence cooperation initiatives, including coordination of the India–Africa Defence Ministers' Conclave and the Indian Ocean Region Defence Ministers' Conclave.

He worked closely with the Ministry of Defence, Ministry of External Affairs, Defence Public Sector Undertakings (DPSUs), Indian shipyards and defence industry partners, providing valuable experience in defence acquisition, stakeholder management and strategic collaboration.

Recent Technical Advisory Contribution

As part of the preparation of the NeuroPi Industrial Intelligence Platform (NIIP) proposal for Mazagon Dock Shipbuilders Limited, Captain Anil Kumar undertook an independent technical assessment of the complete proposal from the perspective of a simulated MDL Technical Evaluation Committee.

The assessment covered the proposal, supporting technical documentation and the Technical Evaluation & Compliance Questionnaire, focusing on naval domain relevance, operational applicability, RFP compliance, technical evidence traceability and evaluator confidence.

The assessment concluded that the proposal is technically mature, well aligned with the MDL RFP and supported by a coherent architecture, while identifying a limited number of recommendations aimed at further strengthening evaluator confidence.
