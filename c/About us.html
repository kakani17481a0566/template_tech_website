Introduction

CargoKite's journey
was inspired by a
passion for kitesurfing.

We show the world it's possible.

CargoKite is a maritime hard tech company based in Munich, Germany. The company was founded in 2022 with the mission of sustainable and at the same time economically viable commercial shipping.

We are convinced that software solutions alone won't suffice to decarbonize the shipping industry. We have therefore developed an entirely new ship class which uses the wind as its main source of propulsion to transport goods to any port in the world.

Milestones

Key
Turning
Points

Achievements

Start from

2019-2020

The idea of CargoKite was born at the Technical University of Munich. Inspired by the airborne wind energy technology, Marcus Bischoff dedicates his academic research to CargoKite and sets out to prove the general technical feasibility of the combination of kites and actively controlled hydrofoils.

October

2020

The team files a patent for the novel ship concept at the German Patent and Trade Mark Office, shortly followed by a European patent.

February

2021

Marcus completes his research work on the trajectory optimization of airborne wind energy systems for the CargoKite vessel concept with a highly promising Proof of Principle: His work shows that CargoKite vessels can realize up to 480% efficiency gains compared to retrofit kite solutions.

April

2021

Based on the research results, the team launches the first version of its proprietary, artificial intelligence powered route planner. The tool calculates the optimal route and precisely determines the Estimated Time of Arrival (ETA) of the vessel based on real-time weather conditions and has has continuously been expanded ever since.

August

2021

The team designs, builds and installs the first stationary hydrofoil system which is successfully tested under different conditions in the research facilities of TU Munich as well as in a local river. This lays the foundation for a series of prototypes to test and analyze the control behavior of the actively controlled hydrofoil system.

August

2022

The team completes the first small scale representation of the overall ship concept. This radio-controlled model (scale 1:20) is being used as a platform to test different control strategies and trim the control parameters based on a wide spectrum of test cases at a lake near Munich (towing tests as well as self-propelled tests).

April

2023

CargoKite provides Proof of Concept with a 9m catamaran equipped with a kite system and proprietary hydrofoils. In the French Atlantic Ocean, the 1.5-ton boat successfully demonstrated its upwind capabilities underlining CargoKites potential to disrupt the shipping industry.

June

2024

CargoKite successfully completes second test campaign of its leisure prototype in Barbate, Spain.

October

2024

The team completes the tender design of its wind-powered 280 TEU catamaran designed to serve the needs of the shortsea and feeder market.
